Who I am

My name is Brian Silvey, but I go by Hiram in honor of my grandfather, and I am currently a 2nd year Computer Science major at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. This website is dedicated to work and projects relating to computer science throughout my college years.

So far, I've completed 7 college level computer science courses, including a more hardware oriented class where we programmed an ARM microcontroller with C++ and Assembly. I've uploaded the code for a couple of projects I worked on in that class to my GitHub page.

I've competed in 1 hackathon so far, where I wrote the code for an Arduino Rev3 to trigger events when certain patterns were clapped. This audio recognition project was the beginning to bigger ideas my college roommate and I have come up with that we hope to develope in the near future. ("Hound" on GitHub)

After taking many computer science courses I've learned that low level systems programming fascinates me more than any other area I have dabbled in thus far. I'm currently in the process of teaching myself Rust, and I'm hoping to experiment with kernel developement on an ARM board sometime this winter break.

My future

The semester of Spring 2015 I will be studying abroad in Seoul, South Korea at Yonsei University in Shinchon. During my high school years I made several good Korean friends, and ever since then I have been interested in the South Korean culture. At the start of my college carreer at UMass, I enrolled in an introductory Korean language class in hopes of learning more about the culture and potentially one day travelling there, myself.
